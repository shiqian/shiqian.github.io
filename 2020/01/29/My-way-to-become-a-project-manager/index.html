My way to become a project manager

發表於 2020-01-29 | 分類於 project management

In the end of 2019, finally I got a job as a project manager, still working in tech domain but not coding.

From 2012 - 2019, it costs me 7 years to find a comparatively clear career path. I am grateful to all people who helped an guided me. I’d like to share my experience to people who are also looking for their career path.

I majored in communication engineer and after graduation I worked as a software engineer from 2012 to 2018. In those years I did enjoy coding, debugging, reseaching but I did not learn new technology out of interest but kinda forced myself for getting/keeping a decent job because I did not know excep coding what I can do. In 2018, I started to explore my potential to try HR relevant positions, and I succeed to get an IT coordinator position at Booking Shanghai. It has no clear job scope, kinda a combination of learning specialist, hr admin, department secretary. I tried my best to grow to be a talent development specialist but it turns out in China BU there is no headcount. I got stucked and had no idea where I would go. In Jan 2019 suddenly I was assigned to coordinate cross-department project and it opened a new door for me. When I walk through my way to become a project manager from Jan 2019 to Nov 2019, I recognise the 4 most important factors: mentorship, framework, practice, confidence.

Mentorship

I am super lucky to have many friends / senior colleagues encourage and coach me through the whole journey.

  • Radoy suggested researching project manager positions, reading job descriptions, identifying the gap between my KSA and job requirements, getting a PMP certificate.
  • Lin recommended me a training agency for PMP cerficate to get me in the door.
  • Yi taught me business metrics, like concepts of uv, pv, conversion rate and how to calculate ROI, etc.
  • Xuan suggested looking for a mentor, figuring out what a project manager does
  • Leoni,sheila,marcela provided bi-weekly coaching
  • Joseph,catherine introduced how payments bu does project/program management (tools, procedures)
  • Xiaolan recommended “Heilmeier’s Catechism”which inspired me a lot during daily work
  • Josie introduced how their technical project manager work with product managers
  • Zheyi involved me in the .cn project for coordination
  • Yini discussed risk management of scrum
  • Jay turned on the switch of my potential to be a PM, showed great trust to let me handle TPM tasks individually, offered me lots of opportunities to make my own way forward
  • Jojo involved me in markeing campain projects, helped me build my first campaign planning sheet and cleary expressed her expecation of the role a PM should play,especially identify risks, resolve communication and collaboration blocks
  • Xiaolu involved me in general project management of tech team, offered me chances to shadow business discussions, and showed me red light report
  • Vivi proposed the project management career and always stand by to support me
  • Ivy & Selena shared me internal opportunities of project managers
  • Timo suggested tips to fight for opportunies to work on project managements and helped on cooridnation of infra summit

Moreover, Victor left me a sticky note of “Best PM in the world”, Shelly always supports me to strive in this way, Teng is willing to do anything to facilitate my PM career.
